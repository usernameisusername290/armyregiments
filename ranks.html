Ranks of the Indian Army

Field Marshal

The highest rank in the Indian Army.

General

Second highest rank, usually in charge of corps.

Lieutenant General

Typically commands a division.

Major General

Usually in charge of a brigade.

Brigadier

Commands a regiment or a battalion.

Colonel

Assists in commanding a battalion.

Lieutenant Colonel

Typically serves as a battalion's second-in-command.

Major

Leads a company of soldiers.

Captain

Usually commands a platoon.

Lieutenant

Assists in commanding a platoon.

Subedar Major

Typically assists in platoon command.

Subedar

Assists in commanding a platoon.

Naib Subedar

Typically supports platoon leadership.

Havaldar

Supervises a section of soldiers.

Naik

Assists in section leadership.

Lance Naik

Generally assists in a section.

Sepoy

Entry-level soldier in the Indian Army.
